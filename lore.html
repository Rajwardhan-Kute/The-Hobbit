Hobbit Lore

Explore the rich history and legends of Middle-earth.

The Creation of Middle-earth

Middle-earth was created by Eru Ilúvatar and shaped by the Valar. It is home to Elves, Men, Dwarves, Hobbits, and other creatures.

Timeline of Key Events

  • Year 2770: Smaug takes Erebor from the Dwarves.
  • Year 2941: Bilbo begins his adventure with Thorin's company.
  • Year 2941: The Battle of Five Armies occurs near Erebor.
  • Year 3019: The One Ring is destroyed, ending Sauron's reign.

Important Locations

  • Shire: The peaceful homeland of Hobbits.
  • Rivendell: A sanctuary for Elves, known for its beauty and wisdom.
  • Mirkwood: A dense, enchanted forest filled with dangers like spiders and darkness.
  • Lonely Mountain (Erebor): The home of Thorin's ancestors, later taken by Smaug.

Legends and Myths

Discover the rich legends of Middle-earth, including the origins of Smaug, the lore of the One Ring, and the ancient Valar who shaped the world.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who is Eru Ilúvatar? The supreme god of Arda, responsible for its creation.
  • What is the One Ring? A powerful ring created by Sauron to control Middle-earth.
  • What is the significance of the Battle of Five Armies? It represents the culmination of Thorin's quest and unites Elves, Men, and Dwarves against a common threat.
